To process 19 categorical and quantitative variables, a Random Forest Classifier (i.e. generally, Random Forest) is implemented with ten trees (similar to a small-scale machine learning model). Here, each tree uses a different portion of the dataset as a training set. A Random Forest is advantageous for large datasets by averaging the individual trees to generate a better fit model. The resulting model captures 84.92% variance. Based on this model, variables are sorted based on relative predictive importance. In other words, ranking helps to determine the variables most significant in analyzing loan repayment status.

Based on this model, variables are sorted based on relative predictive importance. In other words, ranking helps to determine the variables most significant in analyzing loan repayment status.

The top 8 variables are considered relevant, ranging from approximately 8-11% relative importance. Below, these variables and contextual world information are provided.

  1. FICO (‚Äòfico‚Äô) refers to the borrower‚Äôs credit score. Primary factors of credit score include payment history, debt amount, and credit history. A low credit score indicates higher likelihood of defaulting on loans (i.e. a riskier borrower).
  2. Interest Rate (‚Äòint.rate‚Äô) refers to the charged percentage (‚Äòinterest‚Äô) of borrowed money that must be paid to the loaner. A higher rate increases debt over time.
  3. Annual Income (‚Äòlog.annual.inc‚Äô) refers to the borrower‚Äôs annual income. A lower income impedes loan repayment ability.
  4. Days with Credit Line (‚Äòdays.with.cr.line‚Äô) refers to the number of days a borrower has had their credit line. Credit lines are ‚Äúon demand‚Äù loans that allow incremental borrowing, repayment, and borrowing again as long as the line is open. The borrowed balance is charged interest. If the line is open for a longer duration of time, this may impede loan repayment.
  5. Monthly Installment Payment (‚Äòinstallment‚Äô) refers to the mandatory monthly payment the borrower must make.
  6. Revolving Line Utilization Rate (‚Äòrevol.util‚Äô) refers to the percentage of the credit limit that the borrower uses. A higher rate indicates more borrowed money (and debt).
  7. Debt-to-income ratio (‚Äòdti‚Äô) refers to the total debt divided by the borrower‚Äôs annual income. A larger ratio indicates that the income is insufficient for repaying the debt.
  8. Revolving Balance (‚Äòrevol.bal‚Äô) refers to the remaining unpaid amount at the end of the billing cycle. A higher balance contributes to more debt.
